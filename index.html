About Me

Dr. Rahman was born in Bangladesh and grew up amidst its green landscapes, where he completed his B.Sc. (Honours) and M.Sc. in Computer Science and Engineering. He began his career as a full-time faculty member at a public university in Bangladesh in 2009. Since then, he has actively served as a Computer Science (CS) academic for almost nine years, teaching a diverse set of undergraduate and graduate-level CS courses, supervising students, and serving in various administrative roles.

His teaching interests primarily include areas such as Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Explainable AI, System-level Programming, Operating Systems, Data Structures, Algorithm Analysis and Design, and Theory of Computation, among others. He adopts a hands-on approach to teaching and provides students with ample opportunities to challenge themselves so they can consolidate their learning and grow. In 2017, he joined Georgia State University (GSU) to pursue his Ph.D., and in 2019, he began working at the Tri-institutional Center for Translational Research in Neuroimaging and Data Science (TReNDS) in Atlanta, Georgia, where he completed his Ph.D. and continued as a postdoctoral researcher until 2024. At TReNDS, Dr. Rahman and his colleagues focused on building explainability pipelines in neuroimaging, with the overarching goal of gaining insights into brain disorders. Since August 2024, he has been serving as a Lecturer in Computer Science at Georgia State University.

My primary research focuses on Explainable AI (XAI), interpretable machine learning, medical image computing, and computer vision. I am passionate about developing new explainability methods and evaluation metrics, creating AI-driven analysis and interpretation of medical images (including neuroimaging), and ensuring fairness, robustness, and interpretability in machine learning.

Research Interests

  • Explainable AI (XAI): Developing new explainability methods and evaluation metrics.
  • Medical Image Computing: AI-driven analysis and interpretation of medical images, including neuroimaging.
  • Trustworthy AI: Ensuring fairness, robustness, and interpretability in machine learning.
  • Computer Vision: Applications in real-world problems.
